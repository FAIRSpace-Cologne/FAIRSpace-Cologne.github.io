Data Repositories and Infrastructure

Why Repositories?

Data repositories play a crucial role in the research data management lifecycle. They provide secure, organized spaces for storing and sharing research data with the broader scientific community.

  • Preservation: Ensure long-term preservation of research data
  • Discoverability: Make data findable through search interfaces and metadata
  • Accessibility: Provide standardized access to data
  • Reproducibility: Enable other researchers to access data for validation and replication
  • Compliance: Meet funder and institutional requirements for data sharing
  • Citation: Generate unique identifiers (DOIs) for data citation

General Repositories

General repositories accept research data across all disciplines and types. These are suitable for datasets that don't fit into domain-specific repositories or for researchers seeking maximum visibility.

Popular General Repositories:

  • Zenodo (zenodo.org) - Open access repository for scientific data and publications
  • Figshare (figshare.com) - Repository for sharing all types of research outputs
  • OSF Storage (Open Science Framework) - Platform for research collaboration and data sharing
  • Dataverse (dataverse.org) - Network of repositories for data sharing

Domain-Specific and Domain-Relevant Repositories

Domain-specific repositories are tailored to particular research disciplines or data types. They often provide specialized features and metadata standards relevant to their field.

Examples by Domain:

  • Biology/Life Sciences: GenBank, UniProt, EMBL-EBI
  • Astronomy: SDSS, 2MASS, ESO Archive
  • Climate/Geosciences: PANGAEA, NOAA Data Centers
  • Social Sciences: ICPSR, UK Data Archive
  • Chemistry: PubChem, ChemSpider
  • Materials Science: Materials Project, NOMAD

Data Infrastructure

Beyond individual repositories, research institutions often maintain their own data infrastructure to support local data management needs.

Components of Data Infrastructure:

  • Storage Systems: Local storage servers and cloud storage solutions
  • Databases: Structured data management systems
  • Backup Systems: Redundant copies for disaster recovery
  • Access Management: Authentication and authorization systems
  • Metadata Catalogs: Systems for discovering and describing data
  • Collaboration Tools: Platforms for shared access and collaboration

Selecting a Repository

Choosing the right repository for your data involves considering several factors:

  • Discipline Match: Is there a domain-specific repository suitable for your data?
  • Access Requirements: Does the repository support restricted access if needed?
  • Metadata Standards: Does it use appropriate metadata standards for your field?
  • Preservation Commitment: Does it have long-term preservation guarantees?
  • Identifiers: Does it issue DOIs or other persistent identifiers?
  • Funder Requirements: Does it meet your funding agency's requirements?
  • Data Size Limits: Can it accommodate your dataset size?
  • Licensing Options: Does it support appropriate licensing for your data?
