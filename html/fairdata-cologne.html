FAIRdata Cologne

About the FAIRdata Platform

FAIRdata Cologne is a platform based on The Dataverse Project that supports researchers in sharing research data by publishing dataset descriptions (metadata) and displaying it in a metadata catalogue. The platform facilitates harmonized metadata collection through the usage of metadata templates, project-based metadata hosting, and sharing through controlled access mechanisms. FAIRdata Cologne is fully aligned with data management according to the FAIR principles (Findable, Accessible, Interoperable, Reusable). FAIRdata Cologne can serve as a consortium-wide metadata catalogue, enabling large scale research groups such as collaborative research centres (CRCs) in creating their individual project space, customize the provided metadata to the individual needs, and support the definition of data sharing processes within the consortium and beyond. Taken together, FAIRdata Cologne lowers barriers for new collaborations by making ongoing work, expertise, and data resources discoverable and promotes open science to accelerate research.

  • Improved findability and accessibility of research data
  • Harmonized metadata collection using customized metadata templates
  • Publication of metadata including DOI assignment and licensing
  • Definition of data sharing processes within the consortium and beyond, enhancing the reusability and scientific impact of research data
  • Long-term sustainability
