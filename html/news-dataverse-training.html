Dataverse Platform Training

March 25, 2025 Training Virtual Learning Environment

Overview

Comprehensive training session on using the Dataverse platform for data publication and preservation. Learn how to create datasets, manage permissions, assign DOIs, and ensure long-term accessibility of your research data. Suitable for both beginners and advanced users.

This hands-on training provides step-by-step guidance on leveraging Dataverse's powerful features to implement FAIR principles in your data publishing workflow.

Training Objectives

By the end of this training session, participants will be able to:

  • Navigate the Dataverse interface and understand its core features
  • Create and configure datasets with appropriate metadata
  • Upload files and manage file-level permissions
  • Generate and manage DOIs for persistent identification
  • Implement access controls and dataset sharing settings
  • Use Dataverse for long-term data preservation
  • Export and share datasets across platforms
  • Troubleshoot common issues and optimize workflows

Training Modules

Module 1: Dataverse Fundamentals (9:00 AM - 10:00 AM)

Introduction to Dataverse architecture, features, and workflow. Participants will learn about the different entities in Dataverse (dataverses, datasets, files) and how they relate to each other. We'll also cover Dataverse's role in supporting FAIR principles.

Module 2: Creating and Publishing Datasets (10:15 AM - 11:30 AM)

Hands-on walkthrough of creating datasets, adding descriptive metadata, uploading files, and publishing datasets. Learn best practices for organizing files and writing clear descriptions that enhance discoverability.

Module 3: DOI Management and Citation (11:45 AM - 12:30 PM)

Understanding Digital Object Identifiers (DOIs) and how Dataverse integrates with registration agencies. Learn how to generate DOIs for datasets and versions, manage citations, and leverage DOIs for citing and finding research data.

Lunch Break (12:30 PM - 1:30 PM)

Module 4: Access Control and Data Sharing (1:30 PM - 2:45 PM)

Detailed exploration of Dataverse's access control features. Learn how to manage permissions at dataset and file levels, implement restricted access, and use roles for collaborative data management.

Module 5: Data Preservation and Maintenance (3:00 PM - 4:00 PM)

Strategies for long-term data preservation using Dataverse, including file format considerations, versioning, and metadata updates. We'll discuss institutional policies and best practices for data retention and archival.

Module 6: Advanced Features and Integration (4:15 PM - 5:15 PM)

For advanced users: API usage, batch dataset operations, integration with external systems, and custom workflows. We'll explore Dataverse's extensibility and interoperability with other research infrastructure tools.

Who Should Attend

This training is designed for:

  • Researchers planning to publish datasets in Dataverse
  • Data managers and stewards
  • Librarians and research support staff
  • IT professionals managing data repositories
  • Anyone interested in data management best practices
  • Both beginners and experienced Dataverse users

Prerequisites and Technical Requirements

No prior experience with Dataverse is required. However, basic familiarity with research data management concepts is helpful. For the hands-on portions of the training:

  • Access to a computer with internet connection and modern web browser
  • A personal Dataverse test instance (we'll provide access)
  • Sample dataset files (optional but recommended for practice)
  • Zoom client for virtual participation

Training Materials and Resources

All participants will receive comprehensive training materials including:

  • Detailed training slides and handouts (PDF)
  • Step-by-step exercise guides with screenshots
  • Video recordings of all sessions (available for 30 days)
  • Curated resource list with links to Dataverse documentation
  • FAQs and troubleshooting guide
  • Certificate of completion

Instructors and Support

The training will be led by experienced Dataverse administrators and data management professionals. Technical support staff will be available throughout the training to assist with any issues participants may encounter.

Participants will have access to a dedicated Q&A forum for post-training support and guidance for 60 days after the training concludes.
