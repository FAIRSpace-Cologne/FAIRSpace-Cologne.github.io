Research Data Symposium 2025

March 10, 2025 Conference Cologne Conference Center

About the Symposium

Our annual Research Data Symposium brings together data stewards, researchers, and IT professionals to discuss the latest trends in research data management. Topics include open science initiatives, data preservation strategies, and emerging technologies for data sharing and collaboration.

This year's symposium focuses on the theme "Data-Driven Research in the Digital Age" and features keynote speakers from leading research institutions, interactive panel discussions, and networking opportunities with peers from across Europe.

Keynote Speakers

Dr. Elena Martinez - "The Future of Open Science"

Director of the European Open Science Cloud, Dr. Martinez will discuss the vision for a federated research data infrastructure and the role of FAIR principles in achieving truly open science.

Prof. Thomas Schmidt - "AI and Research Data Management"

Professor of Data Science at Technical University Munich, Prof. Schmidt explores how artificial intelligence is transforming the way we manage, analyze, and preserve research data.

Dr. Sarah Chen - "Data Ethics in the Modern Research Landscape"

Leading bioethicist and data governance expert, Dr. Chen addresses critical ethical considerations in data sharing, privacy, and responsible data stewardship.

Parallel Session Details

The symposium features three parallel tracks allowing participants to customize their experience based on their interests and expertise:

Track A: Data Preservation and Long-term Access

Explore strategies for ensuring research data remains accessible and usable for decades to come. Topics include file format selection, migration strategies, emulation techniques, and institutional repository policies.

Track B: Metadata Standards and Interoperability

Deep dive into metadata schemas, controlled vocabularies, and linked data approaches that enhance data discoverability and cross-disciplinary data integration.

Track C: Research Data Infrastructure

Technical sessions on building and maintaining robust data infrastructure, including storage solutions, data transfer protocols, authentication systems, and API development.
