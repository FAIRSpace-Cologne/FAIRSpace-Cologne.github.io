RDM Training

Comprehensive training materials and resources for research data management workshops and courses

Learn through hands-on RDM training and workshops

Structured practical sessions to help researchers build confidence in FAIR data practices.

We offer tailored training specifically designed for researchers working in biomedicine. The aim is to support scientists at every stage of their work by helping them manage, curate, and share research data in a responsible and efficient way.

Practical training across the data lifecycle

The training covers the full research data lifecycle, from early planning and data collection through analysis and publication, all the way to long-term storage. Rather than focusing on theory alone, the sessions address real challenges that biomedical researchers face in their daily work. Participants gain hands-on skills in data organisation, documentation, quality assurance, and sustainable data practices.

FAIR principles and Open Science in practice

A key focus of the training is the practical application of the FAIR principles�making data Findable, Accessible, Interoperable, and Reusable. Researchers learn how to improve the visibility and usability of their data both within their own field and for a wider research community. Open Science approaches are integrated throughout the programme, encouraging transparency, collaboration, and broader sharing of research results.

What researchers gain from the training

  • Greater visibility and impact of their research through effective data sharing and proper citation
  • Improved reproducibility and reliability of scientific findings
  • Confidence in meeting institutional, funder, and publisher requirements for data management and openness
  • Long-term skills for responsible data stewardship and good research practice
