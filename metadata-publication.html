Publishing Metadata

This guide walks you through the process of publishing your metadata records to make them publicly discoverable and FAIR compliant. Learn about the publication workflow, review process, and how to manage published records.

Publication Submission Workflow

Step 1: Prepare Your Metadata

Before submitting, ensure your metadata record is complete:

  • All required fields are filled
  • FAIR assessment score is at least 80%
  • No validation errors are present
  • Content has been reviewed for accuracy

Step 2: Submit for Publication

To submit your record:

  1. Open your draft record
  2. Click "Submit for Publication"
  3. Review the pre-publication checklist
  4. Add any notes for reviewers
  5. Click "Confirm Submission"

Step 3: Review Process

Your submission enters a review queue where:

  • Publishers review metadata quality
  • FAIR compliance is verified
  • Standard compliance is checked
  • Content is assessed for appropriateness

Step 4: Publication

Once approved, your record is published and:

  • Assigned a unique DOI (Digital Object Identifier)
  • Made publicly discoverable
  • Indexed in search engines
  • Added to public API
  • You receive notification of successful publication

Publication Options

Open Access

Your metadata and data are fully public and freely accessible to anyone. This is the default option and strongly recommended for FAIR data.

Restricted Access

Your metadata is public, but access to the data is restricted:

  • Can require login to access data
  • Can restrict to specific institutions
  • Can require explicit approval
  • Metadata remains discoverable

Embargo

Delay public release of your data:

  • Set an embargo period (3-24 months)
  • Data is private during embargo
  • Automatically released after embargo expires
  • Metadata may be visible

Managing Published Records

View Published Record

Once published, your record receives a public URL where it can be accessed. This page shows:

  • Complete metadata
  • DOI and citation information
  • Download/access options
  • Version history
  • Related resources

Updating Published Records

To update a published record:

  1. Go to your published record
  2. Click "Create New Version"
  3. Make your changes
  4. Submit updated version for publication
  5. System automatically assigns new version number

Versioning System

Published records use semantic versioning (e.g., 1.0, 1.1, 2.0):

  • Patch versions (1.0 → 1.1): Minor corrections
  • Minor versions (1.0 → 2.0): Significant updates
  • All versions remain accessible
  • Original DOI points to latest version by default
  • Version-specific DOIs available for citations

Citation and Attribution

DOI (Digital Object Identifier)

When your record is published, a DOI is automatically assigned:

  • Unique, permanent identifier
  • Format: 10.5281/zenodo.XXXXXXX
  • Never changes, even if URL changes
  • Used for citation and tracking

Citation Formats

Your published record provides citation in multiple formats:

  • APA
  • MLA
  • Chicago
  • Harvard
  • BibTeX

Click the "Cite" button on your published record page to copy citations in your preferred format.

Metrics and Analytics

View Record Metrics

After publishing, access statistics for your record:

  1. Go to your published record
  2. Click "Analytics" tab
  3. View metrics including:
  • Number of views
  • Number of downloads
  • Referrers (where traffic comes from)
  • Geographic distribution of users
